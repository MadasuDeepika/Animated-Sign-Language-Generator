Our project aims to bridge the gap between the Deaf people and normal people with the advent of new technologies of web applications, Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing. The main purpose of this project is to build an interface which accepts Audio/Voice as input and converts them to corresponding Sign Language for Deaf people. It is achieved by simultaneously combining hand shapes, orientation and movement of the hands, arms or body. The interface works in two phases, first converting Audio to Text using speech to text API (python modules or Google API) and secondly, represent the text using Parse Trees and applying the semantics of Natural Language Processing (NLTK specifically) for the lexical analysis of Sign Language Grammar.

Why don't more people know sign language?

According to the 2011 census, 2.68 Crore people in India are disabled. Out of these, 7%, ie. roughly 20 Lac people are mute, or have problems speaking and 19%, ie. roughly 51 Lac people are deaf or have problems in hearing. In 2017, there were only 250 registered sign language interpreters available in the country, basically one interpreter for every 28,400 mute or deaf people. With such a huge population disabled with speech, it is almost shameful that there are only 700 schools which teach sign language as an active part of their curriculum. Among the deaf community, a staggering 76% to 89% people do not know sign language.

So why should we learn sign language?

  1. First, it is a unique tool to have which will make you more prepared and more inclusive in your approach towards dealing with and talking to the members of the deaf and dumb community.

  2. Second, learning the language itself is an eye opener and it will show you how people overcome the barriers of speech with actions.

  3. Third, knowing sign language will also keep you in a position to teach others.

  4. Fourth and most important, it is about setting an example for others to follow and starting a cultural shift for the population to be more aware of the problems faced by the deaf and dumb community, and showing basic courtesy by learning a language which is the lifeline for such a large community.
